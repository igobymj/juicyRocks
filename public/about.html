ABOUT

Juice Rocks is a demonstrator of the joyful effects of "juicy feedback" which can enhance the feel and experience of a game dramatically, without any change to the underlying gameplay.

The project was conceived by Michael John (MJ) during his time at UC Santa Cruz and was built with the assistance of professor Eddie Melcer, and graduate students Sam Shields, Weiqiang Chen, and Jonathan Leland. The project is under constant improvement, and feedback is welcome at michael.mj.john@gmail.com!

The term "Juice" or "Juicy feedback" is most often credited to Kyle Gabler et. al. (2005), writing about their experience as students of the Carnegie Mellon ETC:

"A juicy game feels alive and responds to everything you do â€“ tons of cascading action and response for minimal user input. It makes the player feel powerful and in control of the world"

This project takes particular inspiration from the presentation Juice it or Lose it by Martin Jonasson and Petri Purho, preserved in this amazing video, but their Breakout project has been largely lost along with Adobe Flash.*

The intent of the project is as an educational tool for anyone who wants to learn or teach about juice... but it's also kind of a fun toy.

Of course the primary debt of gratitude goes to Ed Logg, Lyle Rains, Wendi Allen and the geniuses at Atari who originated the coin-op classic Asteroids, one of the most perfect games ever created.
